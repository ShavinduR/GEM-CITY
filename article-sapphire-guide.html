How to Choose the Perfect Ceylon Blue Sapphire: A Gemologist's Guide

By Shavindu | Gemologist & Founder

For centuries, the island of Sri Lanka (historically known as Ceylon) has been the world's premier destination for the finest blue sapphires. From the engagement ring of Princess Diana to the crowns of royalty, the "Ceylon Sapphire" carries a legacy of unmatched brilliance and hue.

However, purchasing a premium sapphire requires more than just an appreciation for its beauty. As a buyer, you must understand the gemological factors that determine a stone's true value. Here is your definitive guide to selecting the perfect Ceylon Blue Sapphire.

1. Color: The Ultimate Value Driver

In colored gemstones, color accounts for over 50% of the stone's value. Ceylon sapphires are globally renowned for their vivid, highly saturated blues that do not appear overly dark or "inky." When evaluating color, gemologists look at two primary categories:

  • Cornflower Blue: This is a mesmerizing, vibrant medium-blue with a slight violet tint. It is highly sought after for its luminous quality, maintaining its brilliance even under low lighting conditions.
  • Royal Blue: A deeper, more intense blue with extraordinary saturation. A true Royal Blue sapphire is a collector's dream, offering a majestic and commanding presence without losing its internal fire.

Pro Tip: Always ask to see the stone (or high-quality, unedited videos) in natural daylight. A premium Ceylon sapphire should "pop" with color, not look flat.

2. Clarity: Understanding "Silk"

Unlike diamonds, where absolute flawless clarity is the standard, colored gemstones are formed differently. Minor internal characteristics, known as inclusions, are expected and serve as proof of natural origin.

In Ceylon sapphires, a specific type of inclusion called fine "silk" (microscopic rutile needles) can actually enhance the stone's value. When distributed evenly, this silk diffuses the light, giving the sapphire a velvety, glowing appearance. However, you should avoid stones with large, surface-reaching fractures or dark inclusions that interrupt the passage of light.

3. Cut: Unlocking the Brilliance

A sapphire's cut is critical. Native cutters in Ratnapura have generations of expertise in cutting rough stones to maximize color retention and minimize weight loss.

  • Look for a well-proportioned stone where the color is evenly distributed across the "face-up" position.
  • Avoid stones with a "window" (a faded, see-through area in the center caused by a shallow cut) or extinction (overly dark areas caused by a cut that is too deep).

4. Treatments: The Unheated Premium

As mentioned in our certification guide, over 90% of the world's sapphires undergo routine heat treatment to dissolve silk and intensify color. This is a permanent, universally accepted practice.

However, if you are looking for an investment-grade masterpiece, seek out an Unheated (No Thermal Enhancement) Ceylon sapphire. These stones possess their exact color and clarity entirely by the forces of nature. Because they are incredibly rare, unheated sapphires command a significant price premium and hold their value exceptionally well over time.
